Sorts

Step 1 - Choose Column

Click on a cell in the column that you want to sort.

Step 2 - Home -> Sort and Filter

Go to the Home tab in Excel’s ribbon at the top of the screen. Click the Sort and Filter button on the right side of the ribbon.

Step 3 - Choose Sort

Now you are given a few sort options depending on the type of data in the chosen column. Here, these options are sorting text “A to Z,” or “Z to A.” These options sort the data alphabetically in either ascending or descending order. If the selected column has numerical data, the options to be sorted would be sorting “Smallest to Largest” or “Largest to Smallest.” These sort numerical values in ascending and descending order.


Custom Sorts

Step 1 - Home -> Sort and Filter

Click anywhere inside your Excel sheet’s data. Then go to the Home tab in Excel’s ribbon at the top of the screen. Click the Sort and Filter button on the right side of the ribbon and then choose Custom Sort from the drop down menu.

Step 2 - Column -> ‘Column Name'

On the Sort window that popped up, you can add multiple levels of sorts. Click on the drop down menu under Column, directly right of ‘Sort by’ and then pick the column you want to use to sort by.

Step 3 - Sort On -> Cell Values

In the drop down menu under Sort On, select what you want to use in the sorting. Here we are sorting by cell values, but you can also sort on Cell Color, Font Color, and Conditional Formatting Icon.

Step 4 - Order

In the drop down menu under Order, select what order to sort the data into. For text data, the drop down options are “A to Z”, “Z to A,” and “Custom List…” For Numerical data, the drop down options are “Smallest to Largest”, “Largest to Smallest,” and “Custom List…”

Step 5 - Add Level

Select Add Level at the top left corner of the window to create a new row of sorts. This will add another level of organization in the custom sort. Follow steps 2-4 again to complete the new sort.

Note: You can add as many levels as needed to the custom sort, but the sequence of rows matters and you can move them as needed to perform the sorts in the correct order.

Step 6 - Complete Custom Sort

Repeat Step 5 until you have all levels of sorting completed and then select OK for Excel to execute the sort on the sheet.
